Volleyball Positions Explained


  1. Outside Hitter (Left Side)

    • Where they play: Left front (front row) and left back (back row).
    • Job on the court: Main attacker, serve receive, defense.
    • Known for: Versatility and reliability in attack and defense.

  2. Middle Blocker (Middle Hitter)

    • Where they play: Middle front, often subbed out in the back row.
    • Job on the court: Quick attacks, blocking, net defense.
    • Known for: Quickness, height, and blocking skill.

  3. Right Side Hitter (Opposite)

    • Where they play: Right front and right back.
    • Job on the court: Attack from the right, block against the opponentâ€™s Outside Hitter.
    • Known for: Strong defense and powerful right-side attacks.

  4. Setter

    • Where they play: Right front (front row) and back right (back row).
    • Job on the court: Run the offense, deliver accurate sets.
    • Known for: Leadership, quick decision-making, and precision.

  5. Libero

    • Where they play: Back row only.
    • Job on the court: Serve receive, dig attacks, pass accurately.
    • Known for: Excellent ball control and fast reactions.

  6. Defensive Specialist (DS)

    • Where they play: Back row, as a substitute.
    • Job on the court: Defense, serve receive, keeping plays alive.
    • Known for: Scrappy defense and reliable passing.
